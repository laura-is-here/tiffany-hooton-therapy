About Me

Growing up as a person who was both queer and poor, I have learned firsthand some of the disparities that marginalized groups of people experience and the consequences of those experiences on one's mental health.

After graduating from California State Unviersity, Long Beach, with an undergrad degree in sociology, I decided to pursue my Masters of Science in Counselling at California State University, Fullerton. My dual education in both sociology and counselling as well as the obstacles I faced throughout my own life have informed my current therapeutic areas of interest and practice with my clients.

I believe that social issues affecting LGBTQIA+ people, people of color, and other marginalized groups can be addressed as part of our therapeutic relationship.
